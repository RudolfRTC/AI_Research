## Summary (200-300 words)

Shaw's Metal Cutting Principles is the foundational text for understanding the physics and mechanics of metal cutting. The second edition provides comprehensive coverage of chip formation mechanisms, cutting forces, tool wear, surface integrity, and machinability. The book develops analytical models from first principles, starting with the Merchant circle force analysis and extending through thermal modelling of the cutting zone. Shaw devotes particular attention to the role of material properties -- hardness, ductility, thermal conductivity, and work hardening rate -- in determining machinability. The text establishes that machinability is not an intrinsic material property but depends on the tool-workpiece-process system. Shaw discusses machinability indices, the AISI B1112 baseline system, and the limitations of single-number machinability ratings. The book provides extensive data on cutting forces and tool life for various steel grades, including low-alloy steels. His treatment of the shear plane model and its extensions (Oxley's model) provides the theoretical foundation for relating material properties to cutting mechanics. The discussion of built-up edge formation and its dependence on material composition and cutting speed is highly relevant to understanding how microstructural variations affect machinability.

## Key Findings

1. Machinability depends on the complete tool-workpiece-process system, not material alone
2. Cutting forces can be predicted from material flow stress and shear angle using analytical models
3. Tool wear is governed by adhesion, abrasion, and diffusion mechanisms that depend on workpiece material properties
4. The machinability index (AISI B1112 = 100%) has severe limitations for comparing steels across different conditions

## Relevance to Research Questions

### RQ1 (Conductivity-Machinability)
Provides the machinability-side framework. Defines what machinability means quantitatively and how to measure it. Does not address conductivity directly, but establishes the dependent variables.

### RQ2 (Microstructural Mediation)
Discusses how microstructure (pearlite vs. martensite, grain size, inclusions) affects chip formation and tool wear. Provides the mechanistic link from microstructure to machinability.

### RQ3 (Measurement Methods)
Not directly relevant to conductivity measurement, but defines machining test standards and metrics.

### RQ4 (Predictive Model)
Analytical cutting models (Merchant, Oxley) provide physics-based framework that predictive models should be consistent with.

- **Gaps:** No connection to electrical or electromagnetic material characterisation
