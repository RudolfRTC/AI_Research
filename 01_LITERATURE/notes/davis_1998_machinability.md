## Summary (200-300 words)

The ASM Metals Handbook Desk Edition is a comprehensive reference providing property data and processing information for engineering metals and alloys. The machinability sections provide extensive data on machinability ratings for standard steel grades, including AISI/SAE carbon and alloy steels. Machinability ratings are referenced to AISI B1112 free-machining steel (rated 100%), with ratings provided for annealed and cold-drawn conditions. The handbook covers the effects of composition (carbon, alloying elements, sulphur, lead) on machinability, the influence of heat treatment and resulting microstructure, and recommended cutting parameters for each steel grade. Detailed tables present cutting speed recommendations for different tool materials (HSS, carbide, ceramic) and operations (turning, milling, drilling). The handbook also provides physical property data including electrical resistivity for many steel grades, though this data is not linked to machinability in the text. For this research, the handbook serves as a primary source of machinability rating data and a secondary source of resistivity data that can be cross-referenced. The breadth of steel grades covered enables mapping conductivity to machinability across a wide range of compositions and heat treatment states.

## Key Findings

1. Machinability ratings provided for hundreds of steel grades referenced to B1112 = 100%
2. Carbon content, sulphur, and lead are the strongest composition-machinability determinants
3. Annealed microstructures generally have better machinability than quenched-and-tempered states at the same hardness
4. Recommended cutting parameters (speed, feed, depth) tabulated by steel grade and tool material

## Methodology

- **Materials:** Comprehensive coverage of AISI/SAE carbon and alloy steels
